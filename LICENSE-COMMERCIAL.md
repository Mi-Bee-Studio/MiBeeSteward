## Why a commercial license?

The AGPLv3 is a strong copyleft license: any modification or derivative that is distributed **or made usable over a network** (e.g. a hosted service) must be released under compatible open-source terms. This protects the open-source community but can be incompatible with some business needs:

- Embedding MiBee Steward into a closed-source commercial product.
- Offering a hosted/SaaS version where you do not want to publish your modifications.
- Distributing MiBee Steward as part of an appliance without releasing the full source.

The commercial license removes the copyleft obligation, allowing closed-source derivatives and SaaS use under separate terms.

## Which license applies to me?

You may use MiBee Steward under the **AGPLv3** (free) if **any** of the following is true:

- You use MiBee Steward for personal purposes, internal tooling, or evaluation.
- You modify MiBee Steward and release the modified source under AGPLv3-compatible terms.
- You self-host MiBee Steward and do not expose it as a service to third parties.
- You expose it as a service **and** you publish your modifications under AGPLv3-compatible terms.

You need a **commercial license** if **any** of the following is true:

- You distribute MiBee Steward (or a derivative) in a closed-source form.
- You offer MiBee Steward as a hosted/SaaS service and do not want to publish your modifications.
- Your company policy prohibits AGPL-licensed software.
- You embed MiBee Steward into a commercial product whose license is not AGPLv3-compatible.
